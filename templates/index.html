Guide decisions, not just rankings

The workbench turns uploaded chemistry into review queues, next-step guidance, and session-backed reasoning so teams can decide what to test now, what to learn from, and what to deprioritize.

Use evidence-shaped ingestion

The upload flow supports screening sets, labeled datasets, and measurement-backed scientific data so the system can interpret what kind of evidence you are providing before analysis begins.

Work across durable sessions

Upload, discovery, and dashboard views can all reopen saved sessions, artifacts, and decision summaries instead of behaving like a one-off scoring script.

How the product works

Upload data → validate columns → score or generate → rank → review

The current pipeline uses RDKit features, calibrated classification, novelty-aware ranking, and decision guidance layers that turn raw scores into experimental next steps.

What you can upload

Session-guided ingestion accepts tabular datasets and structure files with SMILES plus optional measurements, labels, molecule IDs, target context, assay names, source, and notes.

What you receive back

Ranked candidates, decision queues, explanations, review-ready statuses, warnings, provenance, and structured artifacts for discovery output, analysis reports, and review workflow summaries.

Who it is for

This interface is for researchers who want support deciding which molecules deserve testing or review next, especially when screening or comparing candidate sets.

Current limitations

Predictions are model-guided recommendations, not experimental truth. Performance is strongest for chemistry similar to the training data, and low-quality or weakly labeled uploads reduce recommendation quality.

What to do next

Start with Upload / Analyze, let the system inspect the evidence structure, then use Discovery for decision guidance and Dashboard for session-wide interpretation.

What this product does not claim

This is not a truth engine, simulator, autonomous invention platform, or replacement for chemists. It should be used to prioritize testing and review, not to replace experiments.
