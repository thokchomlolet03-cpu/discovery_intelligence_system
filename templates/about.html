Method and limitations Interpretation guide

How to interpret the system, when to trust it more, and where to stay cautious.

Discovery Intelligence is a session-backed decision-support system for helping researchers prioritize molecules for testing, review, and learning. It does not replace experiments, domain expertise, or scientific accountability.

Best use
  • Compare candidate sets before limited wet-lab capacity is spent.
  • Inspect novelty, uncertainty, and confidence together instead of separately.
  • Use the workbench to plan what to test next, not to claim experimental truth.

How to read the product surfaces

Each page has a different role in the decision workflow.

Upload Use Upload to inspect evidence, confirm mapping, and start or resume a session.
Discovery Use Discovery when deciding what to test now, what to hold for review, and what to deprioritize.
Dashboard Use Dashboard when judging how much trust to place in the shortlist and what caution signals matter.
Sessions Use Sessions when you need to reopen or compare durable work instead of starting over.

Current pipeline

The system ingests molecular data, builds RDKit-derived features, trains or reuses a calibrated classifier, scores confidence and uncertainty, estimates novelty relative to known chemistry, and ranks molecules by experiment value and review priority.

Confidence

Confidence is the model’s estimated strength for a predicted label. Higher confidence can support screening, but it still does not guarantee real-world experimental behavior.

Uncertainty

Uncertainty indicates where the model is less sure. High-uncertainty molecules are often more useful for learning and review than for immediate, high-trust claims.

Novelty

Novelty reflects how dissimilar a molecule is from chemistry the system already knows. High novelty can be valuable for exploration, but it also means the model may be operating farther from familiar data.

Experiment Value

Experiment value is a prioritization signal that combines uncertainty, novelty, and confidence-related terms to suggest which molecules may be most useful to test next.

Appropriate use

Use the system to prioritize review and testing queues, compare candidate lists, and identify where the model sees high-value follow-up work. It should support planning, not replace lab validation.

Session model

The web product works through durable sessions.

Upload, Discovery, and Dashboard reopen saved session outputs, scientific summaries, and review state so a run can be returned to rather than disappearing after one page load.

Measurement-aware evidence

Quantitative values matter when they exist.

When measurements such as pIC50 or other assay values are present, the system treats them as evidence about the dataset structure and uses them for diagnostics, label derivation, and interpretation.

Trust signals

Trust should rise or fall with the evidence.

Higher trust usually comes from stronger chemistry coverage, better alignment with observed values, and fewer warnings. Lower trust usually comes from weak coverage, sparse labels, or high out-of-domain rates.

What not to infer

A high rank is not proof.

A top candidate is a recommendation for review or testing priority, not a guarantee of real-world performance. Bench validation still decides what is true.

When to trust less

These are the most common cases where the system should be treated more cautiously.

  • Uploaded chemistry sits far outside the system’s stronger reference coverage.
  • There are few or no observed values available for cross-checking.
  • Label coverage is weak or the dataset is noisy or inconsistently mapped.
  • High uncertainty and high novelty are both present, which can make a candidate useful for learning but not reliable for immediate claims.
Limitations

Predictions are model-guided recommendations, not experimental truth. Results should be used to prioritize testing, not replace experiments. Performance is strongest for chemistry similar to the system’s training data, and low-quality uploads or weak label coverage can reduce reliability.
