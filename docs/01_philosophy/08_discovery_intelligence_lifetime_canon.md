# Discovery Intelligence Lifetime Canon

## A Unified Reading and Source Canon for Building Toward Real Scientific Discovery

### Status

This document is a long-horizon intellectual canon for the Discovery Intelligence project. It is not a claim that the current bridge-state system already implements the full discovery architecture implied by these materials.

The current system is still operationally centered on:

- upload
- analyze
- score
- rank
- review
- dashboard

But the deeper goal remains a system capable of participating in real discovery through claims, evidence, contradiction, experiments, belief revision, and durable scientific memory.

---

## Why This Canon Exists

If the ultimate goal is a Discovery Intelligence system capable of new, novel scientific discovery, then the materials worth dedicating oneself to are not just “AI books.”

What is needed is one integrated canon spanning:

- explanation
- criticism
- causality
- uncertainty
- search
- representation
- software
- retrieval
- real domain science

This document presents that canon as one unified list.

---

## The Canon

### 1. *The Beginning of Infinity* — David Deutsch

For the deepest orientation: knowledge growth, explanation, error-correction, and why progress depends on better explanations rather than better curve-fitting.

### 2. *Conjectures and Refutations* — Karl Popper

For the logic of criticism, trial-and-error growth, and the idea that knowledge advances by bold guesses exposed to refutation.

### 3. *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* — Karl Popper

For a harsher, more operational understanding of falsifiability, testability, and what makes a claim scientifically risky enough to matter.

### 4. *The Book of Why* — Judea Pearl, Dana Mackenzie

For the transition from pattern-finding to causal reasoning, intervention, and counterfactual thinking.

### 5. *Causality* — Judea Pearl

For the full causal machinery behind discovery systems that must reason about interventions, structure, and explanation rather than only association.

### 6. *The Art of Statistics* — David Spiegelhalter

For evidence, uncertainty, interpretation, and disciplined statistical judgment.

### 7. *Probability Theory: The Logic of Science* — E. T. Jaynes

For deep epistemic probability and belief under uncertainty.

### 8. *Introduction to Probability* — Blitzstein & Hwang

For probability intuition strong enough to support evidence aggregation, uncertainty, and experiment design.

### 9. *All of Statistics* — Larry Wasserman

For compact statistical foundations that connect probability to inference and modeling.

### 10. *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning* — Christopher Bishop

For a principled ML foundation, especially probabilistic modeling and approximate inference.

### 11. *Probabilistic Machine Learning: An Introduction* — Kevin Murphy

For machine learning through probabilistic modeling and Bayesian decision theory.

### 12. *Probabilistic Machine Learning: Advanced Topics* — Kevin Murphy

For the longer-term layer: deep learning, Bayesian inference, generative models, and decision making under uncertainty.

### 13. *Deep Learning* — Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville

For representation learning, optimization, regularization, sequence modeling, structured probabilistic models, and the foundations of modern neural systems.

### 14. *Mathematics for Machine Learning* — Deisenroth, Faisal, Ong

For linear algebra, calculus, probability, and the mathematical substrate behind ML.

### 15. *The Algorithm Design Manual* — Steven Skiena

For search, combinatorics, optimization instincts, and practical algorithmic judgment.

### 16. *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction* — Sutton & Barto

For exploration vs exploitation, sequential decision-making, and learning from interaction rather than static labels.

### 17. *Active Learning* — Burr Settles

For choosing what to ask or test next when labels, assays, or experiments are expensive.

### 18. *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* — Martin Kleppmann

For state, provenance, storage, consistency, distributed data, and durable information flow.

### 19. *Fluent Python* — Luciano Ramalho

For becoming operationally powerful in Python, not just syntactically familiar.

### 20. *Architecture Patterns with Python* — Percival & Gregory

For repositories, boundaries, domain modeling, and evolving Discovery Intelligence as a serious long-lived codebase.

### 21. *Introduction to Information Retrieval* — Manning, Raghavan, Schütze

For retrieval, indexing, search quality, ranking, and evidence lookup.

### 22. *Knowledge Graphs* — Kejriwal, Knoblock, Szekely

For structured scientific memory, graph-based knowledge organization, and machine-readable scientific state.

### 23. *Graph Representation Learning* — William Hamilton

For learning over relational scientific structures, graph neural networks, and graph-based reasoning.

### 24. RDKit documentation and the RDKit Book

For the chemistry-facing operational layer: molecular structures, descriptors, fingerprints, aromaticity, database support, and Python integration.

### 25. OECD biodegradability test guidelines and review papers

For grounding Discovery Intelligence in real assay logic rather than abstract labels.

### 26. PubChem

For open chemical structures, substances, assays, and linked chemical information.

### 27. EPA CompTox Chemicals Dashboard

For chemistry, toxicity, exposure, model outputs, chemical lists, and prioritization-supporting public data.

### 28. ECHA chemicals information

For European regulatory and hazard context, substance dossiers, and chemical information that matters when scientific claims must be tied to real-world substance knowledge.

## What This Canon Is Trying to Build

This canon is not random. It is trying to build someone who can combine:

- Deutsch + Popper: explanation, criticism, knowledge growth
- Pearl + Jaynes + probability/stats: causality, uncertainty, disciplined belief
- Bishop + Murphy + Goodfellow: modeling and learning
- Skiena + Sutton + Settles: search, sequential choice, experiment selection
- Kleppmann + Python architecture: durable systems that preserve scientific state
- Manning + knowledge graphs + graph learning: retrieval and structured scientific memory
- RDKit + OECD + PubChem + EPA + ECHA: contact with real chemistry, assay reality, and domain truth

---

## Non-Book Primary Sources

Alongside the books, these should be treated as permanent parts of the canon:

- official library and framework documentation for the stack actually used, especially RDKit and the surrounding infrastructure
- test guidelines and assay standards, especially OECD biodegradation materials, because discovery in the starting domain depends on how measurements are actually defined and performed
- chemical and public data platforms, especially PubChem, EPA CompTox, and ECHA, because Discovery Intelligence must ultimately reason over real evidence, not toy datasets
- papers in active learning, causal inference, graph learning, uncertainty, and scientific ML, because books give foundations, but discovery systems live at the research frontier

---

## The Hard Truth

No finite list is literally enough for all possible discovery.

The domain-science layer will expand as Discovery Intelligence expands. If the system later moves beyond biodegradability into broader materials discovery, the canon will need to deepen into areas such as:

- polymer chemistry
- materials thermodynamics
- kinetics
- degradation mechanisms
- assay design
- experimental chemistry

But this canon builds the right mind and the right system substrate for a machine that can eventually participate in real discovery.

---

## Closing View

The deeper goal is not merely to read “AI books.”

The deeper goal is to build a person, and eventually a system, capable of maintaining disciplined attachment between:

- explanations and reality
- claims and evidence
- interventions and consequences
- uncertainty and belief
- search and experiment
- memory and revision

That is what this canon is for.
